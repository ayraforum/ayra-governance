# Chair’s Quick Reference  
*Working Group Meetings*

This quick reference is intended to help Working Group Chairs run meetings that are effective, open, and compliant with Ayra governance policies.

---

## 1. Before the Meeting

✔ Ensure the agenda is published in advance  
✔ Confirm meeting link and logistics  
✔ Review agenda items for scope and relevance  
✔ Be ready to read the antitrust reminder  

---

## 2. Opening the Meeting

At the start of the meeting:

✔ Welcome participants  
✔ Confirm quorum (if required by charter)  
✔ Read the antitrust reminder  
✔ Confirm agenda and time allocations  

---

## 3. During the Meeting

### Keep discussions in scope
- Ensure discussion aligns with the Working Group charter
- Redirect off-topic or duplicative discussions

### Watch for antitrust risk
Stop or redirect discussion if it moves toward:
- Pricing, costs, fees, royalties
- Market or customer allocation
- Competitive strategy or product roadmaps
- Agreements to exclude vendors or competitors

If needed, say:
> “Let’s pause here — this topic is outside our scope and antitrust policy. We should refocus on the technical issue.”

---

## 4. Managing Observers

✔ Observers may attend and listen  
✔ Observers do not vote or introduce agenda items  
✔ Invite observer input only if appropriate and permitted by the charter  

---

## 5. Closed Sessions (If Necessary)

Closed sessions should be:
- Rare and justified
- Limited in scope and time
- Clearly announced

If a closed session is needed:
✔ State the reason  
✔ Limit attendance  
✔ Resume open session promptly  
✔ Summarize outcomes publicly where possible  

---

## 6. Decisions and Actions

When decisions are made:
✔ Clearly state the decision  
✔ Confirm consensus or vote outcome  
✔ Assign action items with owners  
✔ Ensure decisions are recorded in meeting notes  

---

## 7. Ending the Meeting

✔ Review action items  
✔ Confirm next meeting date  
✔ Remind participants where notes will be published  

---

## 8. If Something Feels Wrong

If a discussion raises concerns:
✔ Pause or redirect  
✔ Defer the topic  
✔ Ask for guidance through governance channels  

Stopping a discussion is always acceptable and encouraged when in doubt.
